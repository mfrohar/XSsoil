FAQ

Common questions about soil management

O. Reg. 406/19 is Ontario's On-Site and Excess Soil Management regulation, which came into force in January 2021. It applies to any project that generates excess soil — meaning soil that will be removed from the project area — if that project meets minimum size thresholds. Class 1 projects (generally larger construction and infrastructure projects) have the most comprehensive requirements. Class 2 projects have a simplified set of requirements. Contact us with your project details for a free assessment of your regulatory obligations.
A Qualified Person under O. Reg. 406/19 is a licensed professional engineer or professional geoscientist registered in Ontario with specific experience in soil assessment and environmental site investigations. QPs are legally responsible for the ESMPs and other technical documents they sign off on. The regulation requires QP authorship for Class 1 ESMPs, soil characterization reports, and ESD assessments. Using a non-QP for these documents can result in regulatory non-compliance and project delays. All XS Soil Solutions professionals who sign regulatory documents are licensed QPs.
ESMP costs vary based on project complexity, soil volume, number of receiving sites, and the extent of site investigation required. Simple single-destination ESMPs for smaller projects can start around $3,000–$6,000. Complex multi-destination ESMPs for large infrastructure projects can range from $15,000–$50,000 or more. We provide transparent fixed-fee quotes after reviewing your project information — no hourly billing surprises. Contact us for a free project assessment and quote.
Moving soil without the required ESMP is a violation of O. Reg. 406/19 and can result in stop-work orders issued by the Ministry of Environment, Conservation and Parks (MECP), significant administrative penalties, requirements to retrieve and properly characterize all soil that was moved, and potential Director's Orders requiring site remediation. Municipal stop-work orders can also be issued separately. We strongly recommend addressing ESMP requirements before breaking ground, but we can also help you remedy non-compliance situations urgently.
Soil characterization typically takes 15–25 business days from field sampling through final report. This includes 2–3 days for field work, 10 business days for standard laboratory analysis, and 3–5 days for report preparation and QP review. Rush services with 5-day lab turnaround are available for time-sensitive projects at a premium. Contact us early in your project planning to ensure characterization timelines align with your construction schedule.
Yes, reusing excess soil between your own projects is permitted and often the most cost-effective approach — but it still requires compliance with O. Reg. 406/19. Soil must be characterized, the generating site must have a compliant ESMP, and the receiving site must have a registered ESDA if it meets the regulatory threshold. We help many developers and municipalities build soil reuse programs that significantly reduce disposal costs while maintaining full compliance.
The regulation includes some exemptions for agricultural receiving sites, but these exemptions are narrower than many people expect and include specific soil quality restrictions. Agricultural sites accepting fill from urban construction projects typically do not qualify for full exemption. Rural and agricultural project leaders should seek professional advice before assuming an exemption applies to their situation, as non-compliant fill placement has resulted in significant enforcement actions against rural property owners.
The Ontario Excess Soil Registry is an online platform administered by the MECP where generating sites, receiving sites, and soil lots must be registered for regulated projects. It creates a transparent chain of custody from source to destination and gives MECP inspectors real-time visibility into soil movements across the province. Registration in the Registry is mandatory for all regulated Class 1 and many Class 2 projects. XS Soil Solutions manages all registry filings as part of our ESMP and ESDAR services.
