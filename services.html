Excess Soil Management Plans (ESMP)

An Excess Soil Management Plan is the cornerstone document required for any regulated project generating excess soil in Ontario. Prepared by a Qualified Person, the ESMP identifies the volume of soil to be generated, characterizes soil quality, and establishes approved destination sites.

Our QPs conduct thorough site investigations, review historical records, and coordinate laboratory analysis to ensure your ESMP accurately reflects site conditions and satisfies all MECP requirements under O. Reg. 406/19.

We manage the complete ESMP lifecycle: preparation, registration in the Ontario Excess Soil Registry, ongoing amendments as conditions change, and final record of site condition documentation upon project completion.

When an ESMP is Required
ESMPs are mandatory for Class 1 projects (large-scale construction, infrastructure, and industrial projects). They are strongly recommended for Class 2 projects and any project where excess soil will leave the project area for reuse elsewhere in Ontario.

Key Deliverables

  • Qualified Person authored ESMP document
  • Site investigation report and soil characterization data
  • Ontario Excess Soil Registry registration
  • Approved receiving site documentation
  • Soil sampling and laboratory coordination

Site Alteration Plans (SAP)

Many Ontario municipalities require a Site Alteration Plan before any significant grading, fill placement, or soil removal activity can begin on a property. These plans must demonstrate that proposed changes will not adversely affect drainage, adjacent properties, or groundwater.

Our team is familiar with the site alteration by-laws for municipalities across Ontario and prepares SAPs that meet the specific technical requirements of each jurisdiction — avoiding the revision cycles that cause costly delays.

SAPs prepared by XS Soil Solutions include drainage assessments, erosion and sediment control plans, and all supporting documentation required for permit issuance.

When a SAP is Required
Required when placing fill, removing significant volumes of topsoil, or altering site grades on properties in municipalities with site alteration by-laws. Thresholds vary by municipality — contact us to confirm requirements for your site.

Key Deliverables

  • Site Alteration Plan document per municipal by-law
  • Grading and drainage assessment
  • Erosion and sediment control plan
  • Fill quality documentation
  • Site monitoring and inspection protocol
  • Municipal submission package

Soil Characterization Reports (SCR)

Soil characterization is the analytical process that determines whether excess soil meets the quality standards for reuse at receiving sites. Our SCRs are prepared in accordance with O. Reg. 406/19 sampling protocols and are required for soil lot registration in the Ontario Excess Soil Registry.

We coordinate all aspects of characterization: field sampling by trained technicians, chain-of-custody documentation, laboratory analysis at accredited Ontario labs, and data interpretation against Table 1 and Table 2 standards.

Accurate characterization protects both generating and receiving site owners from liability and ensures that beneficial reuse opportunities are not unnecessarily eliminated by over-conservative sampling programs.

When an SCR is Required
Required for all soil lots being registered in the Ontario Excess Soil Registry, and for any project where soil will be reused at a receiving site. Also required when a project triggers the Phase One or Phase Two ESA requirements under the regulation.

Key Deliverables

  • Field sampling program design
  • Technician mobilization and sampling
  • Accredited laboratory analysis coordination
  • Table 1/Table 2 comparison and classification
  • Soil Characterization Report document
  • Registry-ready data package

ESD Assessments & Reports (ESDAR)

An Environmental Site Disclosure Assessment is required for receiving sites before they can accept excess soil under O. Reg. 406/19. The ESDA establishes the baseline environmental condition of the receiving site and confirms that proposed fill material is compatible with existing site conditions.

Our Qualified Persons conduct records searches, site reconnaissance, and analytical testing as required to prepare a compliant ESDA. We also prepare the Environmental Site Disclosure Report (ESDR) that must be registered in the Ontario Excess Soil Registry prior to any soil acceptance.

Proper ESDA documentation protects receiving site owners from future liability and ensures that the site remains suitable for its intended land use after fill placement is complete.

When an ESDAR is Required
Required for all receiving sites intending to accept excess soil from registered projects. The ESDA must be completed and the ESDR registered before any soil shipments can be received. Exemptions apply to certain agricultural receiving sites — contact us for details.

Key Deliverables

  • Historical records and environmental database review
  • Site reconnaissance and inspection
  • Baseline soil and groundwater assessment (if required)
  • Environmental Site Disclosure Assessment document
  • Environmental Site Disclosure Report (ESDR)
  • Registry registration and ongoing record maintenance

Fill Management Plans (FMP)

A Fill Management Plan provides the operational framework for accepting, tracking, and documenting incoming soil at a receiving site. FMPs are required by many municipalities in conjunction with site alteration permits and are best practice for any site receiving significant volumes of fill material.

Our FMPs establish rigorous truck-by-truck documentation protocols, pre-acceptance soil testing requirements, placement mapping procedures, and inspection frequencies — giving site owners confidence that every load of fill meets their quality standards.

We also provide on-site inspection services and load monitoring programs to ensure the FMP is being implemented correctly throughout the fill acceptance program.

When an FMP is Required
Required by most Ontario municipalities for sites accepting more than 500 m³ of fill. Also required for receiving sites registered under O. Reg. 406/19 that are accepting soil from multiple generating projects simultaneously.

Key Deliverables

  • Fill Management Plan document
  • Truck manifest and tracking system
  • Pre-acceptance testing protocol
  • Placement mapping and grading plan
  • Inspection and monitoring schedule
  • Monthly and final compliance reports

Soil Management Systems (SMS)

For organizations managing soil across multiple projects simultaneously, a Soil Management System provides the overarching framework for consistent compliance, cost efficiency, and reporting. Municipalities, developers with active land banks, and infrastructure owners benefit significantly from systematic soil management.

Our SMS programs integrate digital tracking platforms, standardized documentation templates, trained project personnel, and periodic QP audits to maintain compliance across your entire portfolio of soil-generating and soil-receiving activities.

Mass balance reporting, cost-per-tonne tracking, and regulatory performance dashboards are included as standard components of our SMS programs, giving management complete visibility into soil compliance costs and risks.

When a SMS is Recommended
Ideal for any organization managing three or more soil-related projects concurrently, or any municipality operating a receiving site while simultaneously generating soil from road or utility works.

Key Deliverables

  • Custom SMS framework and governance document
  • Digital tracking and reporting platform setup
  • Standardized ESMP and FMP templates
  • Staff training and onboarding program
  • Quarterly QP audit and compliance review
  • Annual mass balance and regulatory report

FAQ

Common questions about soil management

O. Reg. 406/19 is Ontario's On-Site and Excess Soil Management regulation, which came into force in January 2021. It applies to any project that generates excess soil — meaning soil that will be removed from the project area — if that project meets minimum size thresholds. Class 1 projects (generally larger construction and infrastructure projects) have the most comprehensive requirements. Class 2 projects have a simplified set of requirements. Contact us with your project details for a free assessment of your regulatory obligations.
A Qualified Person under O. Reg. 406/19 is a licensed professional engineer or professional geoscientist registered in Ontario with specific experience in soil assessment and environmental site investigations. QPs are legally responsible for the ESMPs and other technical documents they sign off on. The regulation requires QP authorship for Class 1 ESMPs, soil characterization reports, and ESD assessments. Using a non-QP for these documents can result in regulatory non-compliance and project delays. All XS Soil Solutions professionals who sign regulatory documents are licensed QPs.
ESMP costs vary based on project complexity, soil volume, number of receiving sites, and the extent of site investigation required. Simple single-destination ESMPs for smaller projects can start around $3,000–$6,000. Complex multi-destination ESMPs for large infrastructure projects can range from $15,000–$50,000 or more. We provide transparent fixed-fee quotes after reviewing your project information — no hourly billing surprises. Contact us for a free project assessment and quote.
Moving soil without the required ESMP is a violation of O. Reg. 406/19 and can result in stop-work orders issued by the Ministry of Environment, Conservation and Parks (MECP), significant administrative penalties, requirements to retrieve and properly characterize all soil that was moved, and potential Director's Orders requiring site remediation. Municipal stop-work orders can also be issued separately. We strongly recommend addressing ESMP requirements before breaking ground, but we can also help you remedy non-compliance situations urgently.
Soil characterization typically takes 15–25 business days from field sampling through final report. This includes 2–3 days for field work, 10 business days for standard laboratory analysis, and 3–5 days for report preparation and QP review. Rush services with 5-day lab turnaround are available for time-sensitive projects at a premium. Contact us early in your project planning to ensure characterization timelines align with your construction schedule.
Yes, reusing excess soil between your own projects is permitted and often the most cost-effective approach — but it still requires compliance with O. Reg. 406/19. Soil must be characterized, the generating site must have a compliant ESMP, and the receiving site must have a registered ESDA if it meets the regulatory threshold. We help many developers and municipalities build soil reuse programs that significantly reduce disposal costs while maintaining full compliance.
The regulation includes some exemptions for agricultural receiving sites, but these exemptions are narrower than many people expect and include specific soil quality restrictions. Agricultural sites accepting fill from urban construction projects typically do not qualify for full exemption. Rural and agricultural project leaders should seek professional advice before assuming an exemption applies to their situation, as non-compliant fill placement has resulted in significant enforcement actions against rural property owners.
The Ontario Excess Soil Registry is an online platform administered by the MECP where generating sites, receiving sites, and soil lots must be registered for regulated projects. It creates a transparent chain of custody from source to destination and gives MECP inspectors real-time visibility into soil movements across the province. Registration in the Registry is mandatory for all regulated Class 1 and many Class 2 projects. XS Soil Solutions manages all registry filings as part of our ESMP and ESDAR services.
