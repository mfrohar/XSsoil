Constructors & Contractors

The Challenge

Construction projects in Ontario generate significant volumes of excess soil that must be managed in strict compliance with O. Reg. 406/19. Without proper documentation, soil movements can trigger stop-work orders, project delays, and significant financial penalties — disrupting schedules and damaging client relationships.

Most construction firms lack in-house expertise in excess soil regulations and need a reliable partner who can mobilize quickly, keep pace with construction schedules, and deliver compliant documentation without becoming the critical path item.

How We Help

Pre-Construction

Site investigation, soil characterization, ESMP preparation, and Registry registration — all before the first excavator arrives.

During Construction

QP site monitoring, manifest tracking, receiving site coordination, and rapid response to unexpected soil discoveries.

Post-Construction

Final mass balance reports, Registry completion filings, and compliance documentation packages for project closeout.

Services Used

Excess Soil Management Plans Soil Characterization Reports ESD Assessments & Reports Soil Management Systems

Case Scenario

A developer with five active subdivisions in the GTA partners with XS Soil Solutions for a Soil Management System. Excess soil generated at higher-elevation sites is tracked and reused as engineered fill at lower-elevation receiving sites in the same portfolio — eliminating $800,000/year in off-site disposal costs while achieving full regulatory compliance.

The Challenge

Land developers face soil management challenges at every stage of the development cycle. Acquiring land with unknown contamination history, generating large soil volumes during site preparation, and navigating the regulatory requirements for both generating and receiving sites all create compliance risk and cost exposure.

The opportunity for developers is significant: a well-designed soil management program can convert excess soil from a disposal liability into a valuable commodity, enabling cost-effective reuse between projects and reducing overall development costs.

Property Owners

The Challenge

Property owners who want to accept fill to raise grades, create berms, or improve drainage are often unaware of the significant regulatory obligations that come with accepting excess soil in Ontario. Accepting fill from construction projects without proper documentation can result in contaminated soil being placed on your land — and you bear the liability for remediation.

Municipal site alteration by-laws add another layer of complexity, requiring permits and technical plans before grading work can begin on many residential and commercial properties.

How We Help

Receiving Site Setup

We register your property as a compliant receiving site, prepare the required ESDA documentation, and set up the fill acceptance protocols that protect you from receiving non-compliant soil.

Municipal Permit Support

We prepare Site Alteration Plans and Fill Management Plans that satisfy municipal by-law requirements for grading and fill placement permits.

Protection from Liability

Proper documentation of incoming fill quality protects your property from future contamination liability and preserves its value and usability for your intended purpose.

Services Used

ESD Assessments & Reports Site Alteration Plans Fill Management Plans Soil Characterization Reports

Municipalities

Services Used

Soil Management Systems Excess Soil Management Plans ESD Assessments & Reports Fill Management Plans Soil Characterization Reports

Case Scenario

A mid-size Ontario municipality undertakes a major road reconstruction program generating 40,000 m³ of excess soil annually. XS Soil Solutions implements a Soil Management System: a municipal receiving site is registered and prepared to accept clean fill from the road program, reducing annual disposal costs by $600,000 while creating a local fill resource for future municipal projects.

The Challenge

Municipalities face a dual challenge: they generate excess soil from road rehabilitation, watermain replacement, and utility work across dozens of projects annually, while also operating receiving sites and enforcing site alteration by-laws on private properties. Managing all these soil streams simultaneously requires systematic, organization-wide processes.

Without a comprehensive soil management program, municipal public works departments often spend significantly more than necessary on disposal while missing opportunities to beneficially reuse clean fill from their own projects.

How We Help

Program-Level SMS Design

We design and implement organization-wide Soil Management Systems that standardize processes across all capital projects and public works activities.

Municipal Receiving Sites

Registration, ESDA preparation, and ongoing compliance management for municipally owned receiving sites, including parks, road rights-of-way, and landfill closure sites.

Staff Training & Support

Customized training programs for municipal engineering staff on O. Reg. 406/19 requirements and soil management best practices applicable to public works projects.
